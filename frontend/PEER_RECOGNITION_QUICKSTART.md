# Peer Recognition Quick Start Guide

## Overview

The peer recognition system allows students to celebrate their classmates' achievements and positive contributions. This guide will help you get started quickly.

## For Students

### Sending a Recognition

1. Navigate to **Peer Recognition** in the sidebar menu
2. Click on "My Recognitions"
3. In the "Send Recognition" section:
   - **Search for a student**: Type their name in the search box
   - **Select recognition type**: Click on one of 8 recognition types (Academic Excellence, Helpful Peer, Team Player, etc.)
   - **Write your message**: Describe why you're recognizing this person (max 500 characters)
   - **Choose visibility**: Toggle between Public (appears on appreciation wall) or Private
   - Click **Send Recognition**

### Viewing Your Recognitions

Navigate to **Peer Recognition** and use the tabs:

- **Received**: See all recognitions you've received
- **Sent**: View recognitions you've sent to others

### Recognition Statistics

View your recognition stats at the top of the page:

- Total received and sent
- This week's count
- This month's count
- Breakdown by category

### Appreciation Wall

1. Navigate to **Appreciation Wall** in the sidebar
2. Browse public recognitions from across the school
3. Filter by recognition type using the chips at the top
4. Like recognitions you appreciate
5. Check out:
   - **Trending Appreciations**: Most-liked recognitions
   - **Student Spotlight**: Most recognized students this week

### Notification Settings

1. Navigate to **Recognition Settings**
2. Choose your notification frequency:
   - Instant notifications
   - Daily digest
   - Weekly summary
3. Select delivery channels:
   - Email notifications
   - Push notifications
4. Click **Save Preferences**

## For Teachers

### Viewing the Appreciation Wall

1. Navigate to **Appreciation Wall** in the sidebar
2. View all public recognitions
3. Like recognitions to encourage positive behavior
4. Use filters to see specific types of recognitions

### Moderating Content

1. Navigate to **Peer Recognition > Moderation**
2. Review flagged recognitions
3. For each flagged item:
   - Expand to see recognition details
   - Review all flag reports and reasons
   - Choose to:
     - **Approve**: Mark as appropriate content
     - **Remove**: Remove inappropriate content
   - Add review notes explaining your decision
4. Click the action button to complete moderation

## For Administrators

### School Culture Analytics

1. Navigate to **School Culture Analytics** in the sidebar
2. View comprehensive analytics:
   - Total recognitions
   - Active participants
   - Participation rate
   - Climate correlation score
3. Analyze trends:
   - Recognition trend over time (line chart)
   - Distribution by type (bar chart)
   - Distribution by grade (doughnut chart)
4. Identify top performers:
   - Top senders leaderboard
   - Top recipients leaderboard
5. Use date range selector to filter data

### Monitoring School Culture

Key metrics to track:

- **Participation Rate**: Percentage of students actively using the system
- **Recognition Trend**: Is recognition activity increasing or stable?
- **Type Distribution**: Which values are most celebrated?
- **Climate Correlation**: How do recognitions correlate with school climate?

## Tips for Success

### For Students

- Be specific in your messages - explain exactly what the person did
- Recognize a variety of behaviors, not just academic achievement
- Use the system regularly to build a positive culture
- Make recognitions meaningful and sincere
- Use private mode for more personal messages

### For Teachers

- Model positive behavior by using the appreciation wall
- Encourage students to recognize diverse achievements
- Review flagged content promptly
- Use moderation notes to educate students
- Celebrate high participation in class

### For Administrators

- Share analytics insights with staff regularly
- Set participation goals and track progress
- Recognize classes or grades with high participation
- Use data to inform culture initiatives
- Celebrate recognition milestones

## Safety & Privacy

### Content Moderation

- Any user can flag inappropriate content
- Teachers review all flagged recognitions
- Removed content is not visible to students
- Repeat violations can be tracked

### Privacy Controls

- Students choose public or private for each recognition
- Private recognitions are only visible to sender and recipient
- Parents may have visibility based on school settings

## Troubleshooting

### Can't find a student?

- Check spelling of the name
- Type at least 2 characters for search to activate
- Ensure the student is in your school's system

### Recognition not sending?

- Check that you've selected a student
- Ensure you've chosen a recognition type
- Verify your message is not empty
- Check that message is under 500 characters

### Not receiving notifications?

- Check your notification settings
- Ensure email/push channels are enabled
- Verify your email address is correct
- Check spam folder for email notifications

## Best Practices

### Writing Great Recognition Messages

✅ **Good Examples**:

- "Thank you for helping me understand algebra! You explained it so clearly."
- "Your leadership in our group project was amazing. You kept us organized and motivated."
- "I appreciate how kind you were when I was having a tough day."

❌ **Avoid**:

- Generic messages: "You're nice"
- Sarcastic or backhanded compliments
- Inside jokes that others won't understand
- Messages that might embarrass the recipient

### Building a Recognition Culture

1. Start small - recognize one person per week
2. Look for diverse achievements to celebrate
3. Be genuine and specific
4. Encourage others to participate
5. Celebrate both academic and character strengths

---

**Remember**: The goal of peer recognition is to build a positive, supportive school culture where students feel valued and appreciated. Every recognition, no matter how small, contributes to this goal!
